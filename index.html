Fluency Lab

Goal: Speak without filler words (um, ah, so, like) or awkward silences.

  • Embrace the Pause: When you're stuck, just stop. Silence is more powerful than an 'um'.
  • Breathe Low: Deep breathing prevents the 'choppy' rhythm associated with nervousness.
  • Slow Down: Nervous speakers talk fast. Consciously reduce your speed by 20%.

Structure Lab

Goal: Organize your thoughts into a coherent sequence that is easy to follow.

  • The PREP Method: Point (make it), Reason (explain it), Example (prove it), Point (reiterate it).
  • Past-Present-Future: Talk about how it was, how it is now, and where it's going.
  • The Rule of Three: People remember three things. Give three reasons or three steps.

Rhetoric Lab

Goal: Use persuasive language, metaphors, and storytelling to move your audience.

  • The Three Pillars: Ethos (authority), Pathos (emotion), and Logos (logic).
  • Vivid Metaphor: Explain an abstract concept using a physical object (e.g., 'Life is a staircase').
  • The Call to Action: Always end a rhetorical speech by telling the audience exactly what to do or feel.
