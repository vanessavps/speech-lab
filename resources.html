Training Handbook

Methods of Speech

A collection of professional frameworks to help you master the art of impromptu speaking.

Fluency Lab

The foundation of confidence. Focus on rhythm, breath, and the elimination of "verbal static."

The Power Pause

When you lose your train of thought, resist the urge to fill the gap with 'um' or 'ah'. A 2-second silence sounds like authority to the listener.

Low-Center Breathing

Nervousness pushes breath to the chest. Consciously breathe into your stomach to stabilize your vocal tone and slow your heart rate.

Vocal Variety

Avoid the 'Monotone Trap'. Practice shifting your pitch and volume to highlight key words, keeping the audience engaged.

Eye Contact Hold

Complete a full thought or sentence while looking at one person before moving to the next. It prevents 'darting eyes' and creates connection.

Structure Lab

The architecture of a message. Give your audience a logical path to follow from start to finish.

The PREP Method

Point: State your main idea clearly.
Reason: Explain why it matters.
Example: Provide evidence or a story.
Point: Restate the idea to close.

Past-Present-Future

A universal timeline. Describe how things used to be, how they look right now, and what the vision is for the future.

The Rule of Three

The human brain loves patterns. Grouping your supporting points into three makes your speech inherently more memorable.

Problem-Solution

Define a friction point clearly, describe the stakes of ignoring it, and then present your solution as the logical path forward.

Rhetoric Lab

The art of influence. Move beyond information and start moving people's emotions and perspectives.

The Three Pillars

Ethos: Establish your credibility.
Pathos: Connect with the audience's heart.
Logos: Support your claims with clear logic.

Vivid Metaphor

Explain complex or abstract ideas by comparing them to physical objects. It turns your speech into a mental image for the listener.

Anaphora

Repeat a key phrase at the beginning of successive sentences. It builds a rhythmic momentum that feels inevitable and powerful.

The Call to Action

Never end a speech without a clear 'next step'. Tell your audience exactly what you want them to do, think, or feel next.
